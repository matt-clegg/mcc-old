Litter Picks

Details
By Geoff Orford

Some club members were able to take part in a litter pick on Tuesday 14th Feb but being a week day many could not do so. Here is a link to KMTV coverage of the action. https://www.kentonline.co.uk/kmtv/video/kayakers-join-effort-to-clear-the-river-medway-of-plastic-54791/

There will be other litter picks arranged by the club this year which will be on a weekend. They will be advertised on here and other social media so please keep an eye out and join in if you can. 

Whilst these organised litter picks do help,  a lot more rubbish is not collected so it is worth a bit of effort to gather this on normal paddles. However, please be aware of the dangers and hazzards of collecting items from the river wiithout a picker or gloves.

#shepaddles

Details
By Mark Corti

Maidstone Canoe Club were successful in their application for a bursary from British Canoeing for specifically training female coaches and leaders.

3 of our Members are beginning their journey to become Coaches and leaders.

Charlotte, Micheala and Carolyn are currently paddling and training.

Charlotte has passed her core Coach Modules, completed the White water safety and Rescue Course and completed a 16 hour Outdoor First Aid course to enable her to begin the White Water Leader Course.

Michaela and Carolyn have both completed their Paddle Sport Instructor Courses, which will enable them to run the Discover award for new paddlers, Carolyn has also completed her Paddle Sport Leader Course.

Michaela has also completed her 16 hr outdoor first aid as a stepping stone to further training.
